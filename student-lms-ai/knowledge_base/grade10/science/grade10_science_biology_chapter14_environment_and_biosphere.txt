Chapter 14: Environment and Biosphere

Environment:
The environment includes all living and non-living components surrounding organisms.

Biosphere:
The biosphere is the part of Earth where life exists.

Components of the Environment:
Biotic components:
- Plants
- Animals
- Microorganisms

Abiotic components:
- Air
- Water
- Soil
- Light
- Temperature

Ecosystem:
An ecosystem consists of living organisms interacting with non-living components.

Types of Ecosystems:
- Natural ecosystems
- Artificial ecosystems

Food Chain:
A food chain shows the transfer of energy from producers to consumers.

Food Web:
Interconnected food chains form a food web.

Energy Flow:
Energy flows in one direction only.
Energy decreases at higher trophic levels.

Human Impact on Environment:
- Pollution
- Deforestation
- Urbanization

Conservation:
Protection of natural resources is essential.
Conservation helps maintain ecological balance.

Summary:
- Biosphere supports all life
- Ecosystems maintain balance
- Conservation is necessary for sustainability
