Chapter 16: Natural Resources and Sustainability

Natural resources are materials obtained from the environment and used by humans.

Types of Natural Resources:
- Renewable resources
- Non-renewable resources

Renewable Resources:
Resources that can be replenished naturally.
Examples:
- Water
- Air
- Forests
- Solar energy

Non-renewable Resources:
Resources that cannot be replenished within a short time.
Examples:
- Coal
- Petroleum
- Natural gas
- Minerals

Human Impact on Natural Resources:
- Deforestation
- Pollution
- Overexploitation
- Habitat destruction

Sustainability:
Sustainable use of resources means using them in a way that they are available for
future generations.

Sustainable practices:
- Conservation
- Recycling
- Reforestation
- Use of renewable energy

Environmental Pollution:
Pollution is the contamination of the environment by harmful substances.

Types of pollution:
- Air pollution
- Water pollution
- Soil pollution
- Noise pollution

Effects of pollution:
- Health hazards
- Climate change
- Loss of biodiversity

Conservation of Resources:
Protection and careful use of natural resources is essential for survival.

Summary:
- Natural resources are essential for life
- Sustainable use is necessary
- Conservation protects future generations
