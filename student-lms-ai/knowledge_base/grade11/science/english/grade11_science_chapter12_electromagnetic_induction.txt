Grade 11 Science – Chapter 12
Unit: Physics
Topic: Electromagnetic Induction

Magnetic Fields:
A magnetic field is the region around a magnet or current-carrying conductor where magnetic effects can be observed.

Electromagnetic Induction:
Electromagnetic induction is the production of an electric current due to a changing magnetic field.

Faraday’s Law:
The induced electromotive force in a conductor is proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux linked with it.

Lenz’s Law:
The direction of the induced current is such that it opposes the change that produces it.

Induced Current:
When a conductor cuts magnetic field lines or when magnetic flux changes, an induced current flows in the conductor.

Factors Affecting Induced EMF:
Strength of the magnetic field, speed of motion and number of turns of the coil affect the induced EMF.

Electric Generators:
Electric generators convert mechanical energy into electrical energy using electromagnetic induction.

Alternating Current:
Alternating current changes direction periodically. It is used in domestic and industrial power supply.

Transformers:
Transformers change voltage levels in alternating current circuits. Step-up transformers increase voltage, while step-down transformers decrease voltage.

Uses of Electromagnetic Induction:
Electromagnetic induction is used in generators, transformers, induction cookers and electric motors.

Energy Losses:
Energy losses occur due to resistance and heat generation. Proper design reduces these losses.

Summary:
Electromagnetic induction explains the generation of electricity. Changing magnetic fields produce induced currents. This principle is essential for modern power generation and transmission.
